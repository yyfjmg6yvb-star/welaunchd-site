Getting found on AI

Local SEO vs AI Search in 2026: What's Different and Why You Need Both

Chris · June 2026 · 10 min read

Local SEO and AI search are not the same thing — but they're not as different as people think. Local SEO gets your business into Google's Local Pack (the map results) and traditional search rankings. AI search gets you cited by tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews when someone asks "who's the best plumber near me?" In 2026, both channels matter — and the good news is that almost everything you do to improve your local SEO also improves your AI search visibility. Understanding where they differ tells you where to focus the extra effort.

What is the difference between local SEO and AI search?

Traditional local SEO is about ranking in Google Search. The two positions that matter most are the Local Pack (the map with three businesses that appears for queries like "electrician Birmingham") and organic local results (the website links below the pack). Google's algorithm for these positions weighs your Google Business Profile, reviews, website signals, and citation consistency.

AI search is a newer and parallel channel. When someone asks ChatGPT "which accountant in Leeds should I use?", the answer doesn't come from a traditional ranking algorithm. It comes from the AI's training data, real-time web access (where available), and the structured signals that help AI tools understand and trust a business. Sites with schema markup, strong review content, and clear, authoritative writing appear in AI answers far more often than those without.

The key distinction: traditional search ranks pages. AI search cites sources it trusts. Getting ranked and getting cited require overlapping but not identical strategies.

Does local SEO still matter now that AI tools exist?

Absolutely. The Local Pack still drives enormous volumes of high-intent traffic — people who've typed a specific service and location into Google are ready to call or book. AI search is growing fast but doesn't yet replace that channel; it adds to it.

Think of it like this: local SEO is your presence in the town centre. AI search is your presence in every conversation happening about trades and services in your area. Getting into the Local Pack captures people actively searching. Being recommended by ChatGPT captures people who didn't know to search for you yet.

The businesses that will win in 2026 are those visible on both channels — not because it's twice the work, but because the signals overlap enough that doing one well makes the other significantly easier.

What local SEO gets you that AI search doesn't (and vice versa)

What local SEO uniquely delivers

  • Proximity-based ranking — Local Pack results prioritise nearness to the searcher. A small business three streets away beats a larger competitor five miles out for "near me" searches. No AI tool weights proximity as heavily.
  • Google Maps visibility — appearing on Google Maps as a pinned result drives walk-in traffic and directions requests that AI tools don't generate.
  • Click-to-call and direct action — the Local Pack shows your phone number, address, and hours directly in the results. The conversion path is frictionless in a way AI recommendations are not yet.

What AI search uniquely delivers

  • Question-based discovery — someone asking "who do you recommend for a kitchen renovation in Sheffield?" never types that into traditional Google search. AI tools handle conversational queries that don't have a clean search equivalent.
  • Reputation amplification — AI tools synthesise your reviews, website content, and citations into a reputation summary. A business with excellent reviews and a clear website can appear in AI recommendations even when they're not in the top-3 Local Pack.
  • Topical authority — AI tools cite businesses that publish useful, relevant content about their trade. A plumber who's written a helpful guide about common boiler faults is more likely to be cited as a knowledgeable recommendation than one with a blank website.

The signals that power both channels simultaneously

Here's the practical truth: there's no such thing as "just do local SEO" or "just do AI search" — not in 2026. The core signals that drive both are almost identical:

  1. Complete, verified Google Business Profile — the single most important signal for both channels. GBP data feeds Google's Local Pack algorithm and is a primary source for AI tools recommending local businesses.
  2. Recent, descriptive reviews — reviews affect 16–20% of your local ranking AND are read by AI tools like ChatGPT in 58% of local recommendations. The same reviews serve both channels.
  3. Consistent NAP across 15+ directories — consistent Name, Address, Phone data across the web builds trust for Google's ranking algorithm and gives AI tools multiple corroborating sources confirming your business details.
  4. Clear website with location signals — your NAP in the footer, your location mentioned in page content, and dedicated local service pages serve both channels equally.

The three things AI search needs that local SEO doesn't require

There are three areas where AI search requires additional work beyond standard local SEO:

1. Schema markup — specifically LocalBusiness and FAQPage

Traditional local SEO doesn't strictly require schema markup to rank in the Local Pack. But AI search is dramatically different. Sites with LocalBusiness schema markup are cited in 47% more Perplexity responses. FAQPage schema — which marks up question-and-answer content in a machine-readable format — has the highest citation rate of any schema type in AI-generated answers. If your website has no schema, you're at a significant disadvantage in AI search regardless of how strong your other signals are.

2. Answer-first content structure

In traditional SEO, long-form storytelling and keyword density have historically worked well. AI tools operate differently — they look for content that directly and clearly answers specific questions. If someone asks ChatGPT "does a plumber need to be Gas Safe registered to work on boilers?", it will cite a page that answers that question in the first paragraph — not a page that buries the answer on paragraph eight after a company history section.

Structuring your website content so that every key question your customers ask is answered clearly, early, and in plain language improves your AI citation rate significantly. This is why ChatGPT often recommends businesses that have comprehensive FAQs on their websites — the AI can extract and cite precise answers.

3. Topical content that demonstrates expertise

AI tools apply a version of the "expertise, authority, trust" framework when deciding which sources to cite. A business with a website that includes a blog or advice section — even three or four genuinely useful articles about their trade — is substantially more likely to appear in AI recommendations than an identical business with a five-page brochure site.

You don't need to publish daily. Two or three articles that genuinely answer questions your customers ask — about pricing, process, common problems, or what to look for when hiring in your trade — create enough topical signal to influence AI visibility meaningfully.

The combined approach

Do local SEO well (GBP, reviews, citations, location-specific website). Add schema markup and answer-first content for AI. The investment in the second list is small relative to the gain — and much of the work overlaps with what you're already doing for traditional SEO.

Where to start if you've done nothing yet

If you're starting from scratch in 2026, do these in order:

  1. Claim and fully complete your Google Business Profile — this serves both channels and has the highest single ROI of anything on this list
  2. Build 15+ consistent directory citations — Bing Places, Apple Maps, Yell, Yelp, Facebook, and relevant trade directories
  3. Start collecting Google reviews — at least one per week via a direct review link
  4. Get a website with NAP in the footer, location in page content, and LocalBusiness schema markup (WeLaunchd builds this as standard)
  5. Add FAQPage schema to your website with 4–5 questions your customers actually ask
  6. Write two or three articles about common questions in your trade — be specific, be useful, answer directly

By step 4, you're competitive in local SEO. By step 6, you're visible to AI tools. Neither requires an agency or a large budget — it requires time, consistency, and doing each thing properly once.

Frequently asked questions about local SEO vs AI search

What is the difference between local SEO and AI search?
Local SEO optimises your business for Google's traditional search results — specifically the Local Pack map and organic local listings. AI search refers to getting cited by tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews. Both matter in 2026 because a growing proportion of local business discovery now happens through AI tools.
Does local SEO still matter now that AI tools exist?
Yes — local SEO absolutely still matters. Google Search still drives the majority of local business discovery, and ranking in the Local Pack delivers high-intent traffic. AI search is additive. The smart move in 2026 is to do local SEO well and layer on AI search optimisation — because the core signals overlap heavily.
How do I get my business to show up in ChatGPT answers?
Complete your GBP fully, build consistent citations on 15+ directories, accumulate recent reviews with descriptive content, add LocalBusiness schema markup to your website, and have a clear, specific website that names your services and location. Businesses with structured data and strong review content appear in AI recommendations significantly more often.
What is structured data and does it help with AI search?
Structured data is code that labels your content in a way machines can understand — marking up your business name, address, services, and hours using schema.org vocabulary. Sites with schema markup appear in 47% more Perplexity AI responses than those without. FAQPage schema specifically has the highest citation rate in AI-generated answers.
Can I do local SEO and AI search optimisation myself?
Yes — the core tasks are fully manageable without an agency. Completing your GBP, building directory citations, collecting reviews, and having a clearly structured website are all things a business owner can do. Schema markup is more technical but can be handled by a developer or a platform like WeLaunchd, which includes it as standard.
