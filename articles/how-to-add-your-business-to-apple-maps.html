Getting Found on AI

How to Add Your Business to Apple Maps — and Why AI Search Needs It

Chris · June 2026 · 6 min read

Apple Maps is the default map on every iPhone, iPad, and Mac — and it feeds directly into Siri recommendations, Spotlight search, and increasingly into ChatGPT's local business answers. If your business isn't on Apple Maps, you're invisible to roughly a third of all UK smartphone users every time they search by voice or use their phone's built-in tools. The fix is free, takes about 20 minutes, and most businesses still haven't done it.

Why Apple Maps matters more than most businesses realise

Think of Apple Maps as Siri's address book. When someone on an iPhone says "Hey Siri, find me a plumber near me" or opens Spotlight search and types a business category, Apple Maps is where Siri looks first. Not Google. Apple's own map system.

In the UK, Apple holds around 29% of the smartphone market. That's not a niche audience — that's roughly three in ten of your potential customers using a device that defaults to Apple Maps for local search. And since Apple started integrating Maps data into more of its AI features, an absent or incomplete Apple Maps listing now affects more than just one app.

In 2026, Apple Maps data is used by Siri, Spotlight Search, the Maps app, Safari's "near me" suggestions, and is one of several location sources ChatGPT draws on for local business recommendations.

Apple Business Connect: how to claim your listing

Apple's portal for managing your Maps presence is called Apple Business Connect, and it lives at businessconnect.apple.com. It's free to use and takes a single Apple ID to get started.

  1. Go to businessconnect.apple.com and sign in with your Apple ID (create one free if you don't have one)
  2. Search for your business — Apple may already have a listing populated from third-party data sources. If so, you'll claim it. If not, you'll create one from scratch.
  3. Fill in your details: business name (exactly as it appears everywhere else), address, phone number, website, category, and opening hours
  4. Add your description — 1,000 characters to describe what your business does, who it serves, and what makes it worth choosing
  5. Upload photos — at minimum, your logo and a photo of your premises or team. Apple uses these to make your listing more prominent in search results
  6. Verify ownership — Apple will ask you to verify by phone (automated call to your business number) or by confirming specific business details
  7. Submit and wait — Apple typically processes listings within 3-7 business days
The one thing to get right

Your business name, address, and phone number in Apple Business Connect must be identical to your Google Business Profile and every other directory. One formatting difference — even "St." vs "Street" — weakens the citation signal both platforms rely on.

What to put in your Apple Maps description

The business description is where most businesses write the dullest thing possible. "A friendly, professional service established in 2018." That tells Siri — and your potential customer — almost nothing useful.

Write it like this instead. Lead with your primary service and location, then cover what makes you different, who you typically work with, and what customers can expect from contacting you:

"Sheffield-based emergency plumber covering S1–S14. Same-day callouts for burst pipes, boiler breakdowns, and leak repairs. Gas Safe registered. Fixed-price quotes before any work starts. Trusted by landlords, letting agents, and homeowners across South Yorkshire since 2014."

Every sentence in that description contains something useful — a location signal, a service type, a trust credential, a customer type. Siri and AI tools can extract all of that. Vague copy extracts nothing.

The connection between Apple Maps and AI search

Here's where it gets interesting for AI search visibility. ChatGPT uses multiple data sources when answering questions about local businesses. Google Business Profile is the primary one, but Apple Maps, Yelp, Foursquare, and other directory data all feed into the mix.

This means having a complete, verified Apple Maps listing doesn't just help with Siri — it adds to the overall citation footprint that AI tools use to verify your business is real, active, and trustworthy. A business that exists on Google Maps but nowhere else is a thinner signal than a business that exists on Google Maps, Apple Maps, Yelp, and Yell with consistent information across all of them.

Think of each directory listing as a reference on a job application. One reference is fine. Five consistent references from different independent sources is considerably more convincing.

The Showcase feature: your Apple Maps shop window

Apple Business Connect has a feature called Showcase, which lets you add a promotional card to your Maps listing — a featured image, a title, and a call to action. This appears prominently when someone views your business on the Maps app.

Good uses for Showcase:

  • A seasonal promotion ("Summer garden maintenance — book before July")
  • A key trust signal ("Gas Safe registered · 200+ five-star reviews")
  • A new service you want to drive attention to
  • Your main differentiator at a glance ("Same-day response · Fixed pricing · No call-out fee")

Most businesses that have claimed their listing haven't touched Showcase. That means the ones who do stand out considerably.

How to check if your listing is live

Once Apple has approved your listing, verify it's appearing correctly:

  1. On an iPhone, open the Maps app and search for your business name and town
  2. Ask Siri "find [your business name] in [your town]" — does it pull up correctly?
  3. In Safari on any Apple device, search your business category + location and check if your Maps listing appears in the local results

If the information is wrong (old address, wrong hours, missing phone number), go back to Business Connect and update it. Changes typically take 2-3 days to appear.

What else to add your business to

Apple Maps is one piece of the citation picture. For maximum AI search visibility, your business should also be listed — with consistent name, address, and phone — on:

  • Google Business Profile (the most important one — if you haven't done this, do it before Apple Maps)
  • Yelp UK — cited by Perplexity in local searches
  • Yell.com — UK-specific directory with strong authority
  • Thomson Local — another UK directory AI tools reference
  • Facebook Business Page — with location and category set correctly
  • Bing Places — feeds Microsoft Copilot recommendations

You don't need to maintain all of these actively. You need to claim them, fill them in correctly, and ensure the information is consistent. That's a one-time job per platform, not an ongoing commitment.

Frequently asked questions

How do I add my business to Apple Maps?

Add your business to Apple Maps via Apple Business Connect at businessconnect.apple.com. Create a free account, search for your business (Apple may already have a listing), and claim or create it. Verify ownership by phone or by confirming details. The full process takes about 20 minutes and listings typically go live within 3-7 days.

Does Apple Maps affect local SEO?

Apple Maps is a citation source that contributes to your local SEO authority, particularly for Siri searches on Apple devices. More significantly, Apple Maps data is one of several sources ChatGPT draws on when recommending local businesses. A complete, verified Apple Maps listing makes you visible to iPhone users searching by voice or using Spotlight.

Is Apple Business Connect free?

Yes, Apple Business Connect is completely free. You can claim your business listing, add photos, set your hours, update your description, and manage your Apple Maps presence at no cost.

Why is my business not showing on Apple Maps?

If your business isn't on Apple Maps, it either hasn't been added or hasn't been claimed and verified. Go to businessconnect.apple.com, search for your business name and address, and claim the existing listing or create a new one. After verification, your listing typically appears within 5-7 days.
