Schema Markup for Local Businesses: The 2026 Guide to Getting Found by AI

Chris · June 2026 · 7 min read

Schema markup is invisible code on your website that tells AI search engines exactly what your business is, what you do, where you are, and who you serve — without them having to guess. Websites with proper schema markup are 2.5 times more likely to be cited in AI-generated answers. Most local business websites have none. This article explains what schema is, why it matters so much in 2026, and how to get it sorted without needing a degree in computer science.

What schema markup actually is (in plain English)

Imagine you print a flyer for your business. A human reads it and understands everything — your name, what you do, your phone number, your area. Now imagine a robot trying to read the same flyer. It can see the words, but it can't be completely sure which bit is your name, which is your phone number, or whether "Birmingham" means you're based there or just serve there.

Schema markup is basically a set of labels you add to your website that removes all that guesswork. You're saying: "This is our business name. This is our address. This is our phone number. These are our opening hours. This is our service area." AI reads those labels directly, instantly, and reliably.

"Without schema markup, AI has to guess what your website is about. With it, you walk in already introduced."

Why schema matters so much more in 2026

A couple of years ago, schema was mostly a nice-to-have that gave you better-looking search results — star ratings showing up under your listing, that kind of thing. In 2026 it's something different.

Google's AI (Gemini-powered) now uses schema markup to verify the claims on your website, understand how your business relates to other entities, and assess whether you're a credible source before citing you in an AI Overview. Perplexity and ChatGPT do similar checks. Schema is now a direct input into whether AI recommends you at all.

The data backs this up: websites with correct LocalBusiness schema are 2.5 times more likely to appear in AI-generated local answers. That's not a rounding error — it's roughly the difference between showing up and not showing up.

The key stat

The five schema types that actually matter for local businesses

You don't need to implement every type of schema that exists. For most local businesses, five types cover nearly everything:

1. LocalBusiness (or a more specific subtype)

This is the big one. It tells search engines and AI exactly what kind of business you are, where you're located, your hours, your phone number, your service area. If you do nothing else, do this. There are dozens of subtypes — Plumber, Restaurant, Dentist, LegalService — and using the most specific one available for your business type is better than the generic LocalBusiness.

2. Organization

This one establishes your business as a named entity in the knowledge graph — think of it as your business's official ID card on the internet. It includes your logo, social profiles, and founding details. It helps AI connect all mentions of your business name across the web and verify they're all referring to the same entity.

3. FAQPage

If you have a FAQ section on your website (and you should), marking it up with FAQPage schema makes those questions and answers directly readable by AI. When someone asks an AI tool a question that your FAQ answers, AI can pull your answer verbatim. This is one of the fastest routes to getting cited.

4. Service

For businesses that offer specific services, Service schema lets you describe each one in detail — what it is, what it costs (if you want to be that transparent), who it's for. This is particularly valuable for trades businesses, professionals, and any service with a specific name or category.

5. Review / AggregateRating

This connects your on-site testimonials and reviews to your schema, making them machine-readable. It contributes to the trust signals AI looks at when deciding whether to recommend you.

How to check if your site already has schema

Before you do anything else, check what's already there. Google has a free tool called the Rich Results Test (search for it). Paste in your website URL and it will show you any schema that's already implemented, and flag any errors.

Most small business websites I look at have either no schema at all, or a bare minimum that was added automatically by their website builder and hasn't been configured properly. Both situations are worth fixing.

How to get schema added to your website

There are three routes depending on how your website was built:

If you're on WordPress

There are several good schema plugins — Yoast SEO (with the local add-on), Schema Pro, and Rank Math all handle LocalBusiness schema well. Install one, fill in your business details, and the plugin generates and adds the code for you. No coding needed.

If you're on Shopify

Shopify adds basic product schema automatically, but it doesn't handle LocalBusiness schema for physical stores. You'll need an app (several are free in the Shopify App Store) or a small snippet of JSON-LD code added to your theme's head section.

If someone else built your website

Send them this article. Ask them to implement LocalBusiness, Organization, and FAQPage schema. It's usually a few hours of work — not a major project. If they don't know what schema markup is, that's useful information about whether they're the right person to be maintaining your website in 2026.

What to ask for

If you're working with a web developer or agency, ask specifically for LocalBusiness schema, Organization schema, and FAQPage schema to be implemented using JSON-LD format. JSON-LD is the format Google and most AI tools prefer — it's added in the <head> of your pages, not buried in your content.

What schema won't do

Worth being clear about this. Schema markup makes your information machine-readable and boosts your chances of being cited by AI. It doesn't magic you into the top of Google overnight, and it won't compensate for a website that has no useful content. It works best as part of a complete foundation: a properly written website with clear service pages, a solid GBP, and genuine reviews. Schema is the final layer that makes all that work legible to AI.

The bottom line

Schema markup is one of the highest-leverage, lowest-effort improvements most local business websites can make in 2026. It takes a few hours to implement properly, it doesn't require ongoing maintenance, and it directly improves your chances of being cited by every major AI search tool. The businesses that have it are 2.5 times more likely to show up in AI answers. The ones that don't are hoping AI figures it out anyway. Most of the time, it doesn't.
